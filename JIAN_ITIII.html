CHAPTER IV:INTRODUCTION TO COMPUTERSYSTEMS

On this topic, I learned about the 4 components of a computer system: hardware, software, dataware, and peopleware. Hardware encompasses the tangible, physical components of a computer system, the intricate network of electronic circuits and mechanical parts that bring a computer to life.Software is the intangible counterpart to hardware, the collection of instructions and programs that govern the computer's behavior.While hardware and softwar e form the backbone of a computer system, it is peopleware, the human element, that breathes life into it.Dataware, the vast repository of information stored within a computer system, provides the raw material for processing and analysis.

CHAPTER V:BASIC CONCEPT OF COMPUTER SECURITY

Computer security is the practice of protecting computers and information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction. It is a broad term that encompasses a wide range of technologies, processes, and practices designed to protect systems, networks, and data from a variety of threats.
